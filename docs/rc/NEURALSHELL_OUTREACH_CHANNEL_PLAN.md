# NeuralShell Outreach Channel Plan

This document defines the core channels for our first 10 buyer conversations, why they matter, and how to use them effectively.

## 1. Direct DMs (Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
- **Why it matters**: Fastest path to high-profile IFBs. Allows for personal, context-aware outreach.
- **Message Style**: Professional but casual. Focus on "shared interest" in local-AI or privacy.
- **What NOT to do**: Use generic "bot-like" greetings. Don't lead with the price ($99); lead with the "Review Boundaries" hook.

## 2. Niche Discord Communities (Ollama, LocalLLaMA)
- **Why it matters**: These communities are full of users who are *already* solving the problems NeuralShell addresses.
- **Message Style**: Peer-to-peer. Helpful, technical, and curiosity-driven.
- **What NOT to do**: Drop links in general channels without permission. Avoid "marketing-speak"; talk like a developer.

## 3. Reddit (r/LocalLLaMA, r/SelfHosted)
- **Why it matters**: Massive concentration of the target audience. High transparency and "no-BS" culture.
- **Message Style**: High-signal, low-hype. Focus on the "Sealed Golden Master" and reproducibility.
- **What NOT to do**: Spam your own product in unrelated threads. Do not try to "astroturf" or fake interest.

## 4. Personal Network (Email/Phone)
- **Why it matters**: Highest trust. These are your "Warm 3" prospects. They are most likely to provide honest feedback and early sales.
- **Message Style**: Highly personal. "I'm building this thing and wanted your thoughts."
- **What NOT to do**: Treat them like strangers. Don't hide the "Alpha" status; be honest about the journey.

## 5. GitHub (Issue comments/DMs)
- **Why it matters**: Targeting people who have expressed specific pain points in other local-AI projects.
- **Message Style**: Technical and problem-solving. "I saw you were struggling with [Issue] in [Project]. We built a terminal that solves this via [Feature]."
- **What NOT to do**: Spam project issues with links. Only reach out if you have a genuine solution to their specific problem.

---

## Channel Priority Mix
1. **Personal Network**: 30% (The Warm 3)
2. **Twitter/X DMs**: 40% (Targeted IFBs)
3. **Discord/Reddit**: 30% (Community Leaders)
