# NeuralShell Close Path

This document provides the internal guidance for closing a sale once a prospect has expressed interest in the "Operator" tier.

## 1. The "Single Link" Rule
- **Guidance**: When a prospect is ready to buy, send **one link and only one link**.
- **The Link**: `https://gumroad.com/l/neuralshell-operator`
- **Why**: Multiple links or a "choose your own adventure" checkout flow increases friction and reduces conversion.

## 2. The "30-Second Answer" Rule
- **Guidance**: If they ask a technical or pricing question during the "Close" phase, answer it directly and immediately (within 4-6 hours).
- **Format**:
  - "Yes, it supports Ollama out of the box."
  - "No, there's no subscription. It's one-time."
  - "Yes, your data stays local."

## 3. The "Visual Proof" Assets
- **Guidance**: If they seem hesitant, send **one high-signal screenshot** or a **short video clip** of the feature they asked about.
- **Top 3 Assets**:
  - **The Gated Decision**: Shows the app halting and asking for approval.
  - **The Persistence Panel**: Shows a list of encrypted, saved workflows.
  - **The Integrity Audit**: Shows the SHA256 verification and "Green" build status.

## 4. Avoiding "The Info Dump"
- **Guidance**: Do not send the full documentation pack, GitHub history, or the Golden Master manifest *before* they purchase unless they specifically ask. 
- **Focus**: Stay on the **Value** (Control and Sovereignty) and the **Outcome** (Secure, Autonomous workflows).

## 5. Moving to Payment
- **The Script**: "Awesome, sounds like it's a great fit for your setup. You can secure your node and get immediate access here: https://gumroad.com/l/neuralshell-operator. I'll keep an eye out for the order and get you sorted in the Discord ASAP."

---

## Red Flags (When NOT to close)
- **The "Over-Demanding" User**: If they are already asking for 10 custom features before buying, they may be a high-support-burden user. Tread carefully.
- **The "Wrong OS" User**: If they are on Mac/Linux, do not close the sale. Be honest and add them to the "Beta Waitlist" instead.
